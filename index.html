DCF Intranet Site

I played a major role in converting the old DCF intranet site from Sharepoint Designer to Drupal. As part of the core team I worked on the interface design, color scheme, and template chosen for the website. I was also in charge of meeting with clients to make a game plan for converting their content. Once the content was drafted on the new site I presented it to them and we discussed ways that it could be improved or changed and then implemented those changes.

Amongst the many projects that the new site created was a need for an online employee recognition board. I designed and developed the form used for recognizing a coworker. On submission the recognized employee would receive an email that contained the praise or thanks from their coworker. Another email was sent to the recognized employee's supervisor letting them know that one of their employees had done a good job. The praises were also posted to a feed on the intranet home page for all to see.

DCF SPARC

While working for the Department of Children and Families (DCF) I had the opportunity to work with a team to upgrade the SPARC website from a predominantly HTML site to a content management system (CMS).

I was part of a team of three that designed and developed the new site. I produced the new web pages based on content from the old site that our client indicated was still valid. I also contributed to the color scheme. Joe Harrison implemented the Drupal template/theme and setup the modules. Phil Staley handled the search functionality and project management while aiding both Joe and me in any area necessary.

The main goal of the project was to have a simple design that allowed external users to find and access reports and documentation with ease.

Ramen Re-design

I was tasked with re-designing Maruchan Ramen packaging for a school project. My instructor acted as the client. I made a few sketched drafts of potential designs we could develop and she whittled down what she liked and what she didn't, sometimes being quite vague as a client might be.

The final design was chosen to target a youth demographic that is interested in the anime genre. The design can be used across all the Ramen flavors while a simple change of the packaging color would help indicate the flavor visually.
